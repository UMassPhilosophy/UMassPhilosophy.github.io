Welcome to UMass!

On behalf of the UMass Department of Philosophy, I want to begin by extending you a warm welcome. We are really happy to have you aboard.

The purpose of this site is to provide you with some important information you need to know as a graduate student in our program and to point you where you can find more information if needed. I’ve also included some additional information you may find useful as you prepare to begin your time at UMass.

The amount of information you will find here might seem overwhelming, but it really is important, and some of it essential to such things as getting paid and accessing the building. So please take your time to go over it and do not hesitate to reach out with any questions you may have. This page should give you a good sense of who to ask about various topics, but feel free to ask me anything.

I recommend you start by looking at the checklists. Each item on those lists contains links to additional information about each of the tasks, so that rather than reading everything on this page, you can just take one task at a time.

In addition to the items on the checklists, all of which amount to tasks you definitely need to do either before the start of the school year or before the end of the first week of classes, I have put together a page with additional information you may find helpful as you prepare your move to Amherst, including some information about housing.

Fall semester begins on Tuesday, September 3th, but you should make sure to arrive in the area a week or more before that. You are expected on campus for a departmental orientation event on Wednesday, August 28th. In addition, there is a Graduate School orientation event on Friday, August 30th that I encourage all of you to attend. International students are also required to attend an additional orientation event on Thursday, August 29th. Finally, note that we will be hosting a start-of-the-year Departmental meeting, most likely on Friday, September 6th, which I very much hope all of you can attend. We will “introduce” each of you to the rest of the department at this meeting. Check your email for the exact date, time and location.

Best of luck to you as you make this important transition.

Yours,

Alejandro
