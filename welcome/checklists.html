Things you need to do

First of all, you should review the “New Graduate Student Checklist” created by the Graduate School. It contains information about setting up your UMass email account, updating your information on SPIRE, getting your UCard, completing your immunization record, obtaining health insurance, etc.

If you are an international student, you should have already started working with the International Programs Office (IPO) to complete your I-20 and GLACIER forms and to obtain your visa. If you haven’t yet, please contact them as soon as possible.

To do as soon as possible

  • Make sure that all final transcripts necessary for matriculation have been submitted
  • Activate your University IT account
  • Make sure you have access to your University email account and start monitoring it on a regular basis
  • Accept the terms and conditions of your employment/funding in SPIRE (more)
  • Sign fellowship or other funding contract, if applicable (more)
  • Complete the HR hiring packet (if applicable) at least two weeks before the beginning of the Fall semester
  • Inform Beth of when you’ll be arriving in the area
  • Upload a photo, as well as a copy of a government issued ID, to the UCard photo upload website (note that you will need your NetID and password for this )
  • Register for the proseminar and a preliminary set of other courses (more)
  • Setup eduroam on your computer or tablet in order to have access to wireless internet on campus
  • Familiarize yourself with the program regulations
  • Familiarize yourself with the policies and procedures described on this page

To do during the week of August 26th

  • Pickup your UCard by Tuesday, August 27th (note that you no longer need to make an appointment: simply show up at the UCard office during their business hours and pick it up)
  • Attend the Department’s Orientation Event on Wednesday, August 28thh
  • For international students: attend the mandatory International Student Orientation on Thursday, August 29th
  • Attend WUGS, the Graduate School Orientation event on Friday, August 30th.
  • Schedule a one-on-one meeting with the GPD for the first week of classes
  • Schedule your first meeting with your advisor for the first week of classes
  • Make sure you have access to your office and to the building

To do on the first week of classes

  • Meet with your advisor
  • Meet with the GPD
