For International Students

You should have already begun working with the International Programs Office (IPO) to complete your I-20 and obtain your visa. If you haven’t yet, you should start as soon as possible. You can find contact information on the IPO’s website.

Please defer to IPO in matters pertaining to your visa and immigration; they can tell you what documents are necessary, and when and how to submit them. The IPO’s website also contains a lot of other information that may be useful to international students. They also publish a handbook for International Students, which you can find here.

You should be aware that the process of obtaining a visa can be time-consuming and complicated, and may involve:

  • Arranging a consular visit to obtain your visa and immigration paperwork.
  • Providing your immunization record, either at the consulate or at the border when entering. Make sure your vaccinations are up to date. (It is possible you will be allowed to catch up on vaccinations after arrival; this can be done through University Health Services, UHS.)
  • Demonstrating a certain amount of financial assets to obtain your I-20 in excess of the amount of money UMass pays (tuition waiver plus fellowship or TA-ship). Typically this ranges from $3000–$8000 that you will need to show in your bank account. (After approval it needn’t remain there.)

Before you contact the consulate, you should make sure that both the tuition waiver and any fellowship/TA-ship are reflected on your I-20.

You will also need to fill out tax information through the University’s GLACIER tax system. You should receive an email from Human Resources (HR) about this. Every country has its own tax agreement with the U.S. (the amount withheld will vary by country). HR can help you with filling out the form.

International students receiving fellowship money need an international tax identification number (ITIN). (A social security number, SSN, is only needed for TAs/TOs.) You will need to go through IPO for the ITIN, with the help of the University Controller’s Office. This process takes a long time, so get on this ASAP by talking to IPO. Beth can also help direct you. You will need your Visa, I-20, most recent I-94, and your passport. Note that the Controller’s Office is not on campus. Their website is here: https://www.umass.edu/controller/

Contact the IPO, HR and/or Beth with any questions throughout this process.
