Departmental and University Procedures and Policies

As a graduate student in the Department, you are expected to be familiar with the program’s regulations. It is thus advisable that you read over them before the beginning of your first year and that you contact your advisor or the GPD if you have any questions about them.

In addition to the official regulations, there are a few Departmental and University policies and procedures you should be aware of.

Email

Each incoming student will be provided with an University email account. It is crucial that you monitor this account on a regular basis, as that is the address that will be used by the Department and the University for all official communication. (If you prefer, you can have all emails sent to your UMass account forwarded to another account.)

Your UMass email address has been or soon will be added to our graduate student mailing list: philos-all-grad@groups.umass.edu

Messages sent to that address should reach all the students in the program.

Office assignments, keys, and mailboxes

You will be sharing an office with one or more other graduate students. The assignments will be sent out in July to the graduate student mailing list.

Once you pick up your UCard, let Beth Know so she can request as door access to the building, which is needed to enter South College in the evening and on weekends, when it’s closed to visitors.

Every graduate student is also assigned a mailbox in the Philosophy Department’s copier/mail room in E305 South College. It is important that you check this on a regular basis. Any Department member can tell you the access code to the room (for when the door is closed/locked).

Registering for classes

You should enroll in courses as soon as possible, because this is needed to trigger the mechanisms that lead to your financial aid. You can change your courses without penalty until the end of add/drop so your choices need not be final. Registration continues through Monday, September 16th, 2024, the final day of the add/drop period.

All first-year students are required to take the Proseminar (Philosophy 700) during the Fall of their first year. Typically, students take five other courses during their their first year, or sometimes six if they are not teaching during their first two semesters. We strongly encourage you to discuss your choice of courses with your advisor or the GPD by the end of the first week of classes.

A list of philosophy graduate courses for Fall 2025 can be found on the Department website under “Graduate Courses”.

In order to enroll in courses, you need to make use of SPIRE. The Graduate School provides information on enrollment here and here.

Contracts

Whether or not you are employed by the university this coming semester, you must agree to the Terms and Conditions online. To do this, you must log on to SPIRE using the student role and click Graduate Employment Terms in the To Do List details. You must make sure that your browser security settings allow pop-ups on SPIRE.

If you are receiving a fellowship, Beth will complete your fellowship contract before July 1st. Please check your UMass email to watch for her email requesting your signature on the contract which can be done electronically. International students are not required to sign the contract, but she will forward a copy of the completed contract for your records.

You will need to enroll in Excess Express through your SPIRE financials page (using a bank account) to be eligible to receive your funding as a direct deposit to your bank.

New TAs/TOs must complete the HR hiring packet at least two weeks prior to the start of the semester to receive paychecks. The packet and instructions for completing it are available here:

https://www.umass.edu/hr/documents/hiring-forms-new-graduate-student-employees

Tuition waivers and bills

Most forms of funding offered by the Department (fellowships, most TA/TO-ships) carry tuition waivers with them.

You will get a bill from the Bursar’s Office. Do not pay it immediately. It takes a while for your tuition waiver to be applied to your account and for you to get your funding. If you have any questions about your bill ask Beth first before contacting the Bursar’s office.
